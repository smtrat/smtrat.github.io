1 Polynomials {#polynomials}
2 =====
3 
4 In order to represent polynomials, we define the following hierarchy of classes:
5 
6 - Coefficient: Represents the numeric coefficient..
7 - Variable: Represents a variable.
8 - Monomial: Represents a product of variables.
9 - Term: Represents a product of a constant factor and a Monomial.
10 - MultivariatePolynomial: Represents a polynomial in multiple variables with numeric coefficients.
11 
12 We consider these types to be embedded in a hierarchy like this:
13 
14 - MultivariatePolynomial
15  - Term
16  - Monomial
17  - Variable
18  - Coefficient
19 
20 We will abbreviate these types as C, V, M, T, MP.
21 
22 ## UnivariatePolynomial
23 Additionally, we define a UnivariatePolynomial class.
24 It is meant to represent either a univariate polynomial in a single variable, or a multivariate polynomial with a distinguished main variable.
25 
26 In the former case, a number type is used as template argument. We call this a _univariate polynomial_.
27 
28 In the latter case, the template argument is instantiated with a multivariate polynomial. We call this a _univariately represented polynomial_.
29 
30 A UnivariatePolynomial, regardless if univariate or univariately represented, is mostly compatible to the above types.

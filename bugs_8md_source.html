1 Finding and Reporting Bugs {#bugs}
2 ==============================
3 
4 This page is meant as a guide for the case that you find a bug or any unexpected behaviour.
5 We consider any of the following events a (potential) bug:
6 - SMT-RAT crashes.
7 - A library used through SMT-RAT crashes.
8 - SMT-RAT gives incorrect results.
9 - SMT-RAT does not terminate (for reasonably sized inputs).
10 - SMT-RAT does not provide a method or functionality that should be available according to this documentation.
11 - SMT-RAT does not provide a method or functionality that you consider crucial or trivial for some of the datastructures.
12 - Compiling SMT-RAT fails.
13 - Compiling your code using SMT-RAT fails and you are pretty sure that you use SMT-RAT according to this documentation.
14 
15 In any of the above cases, make sure that:
16 - You have installed all necessary @ref dependencies in the required versions.
17 - You work on something that is similar to a system listed as supported platform at @ref getting_started.
18 - You can (somewhat reliably) reproduce the error with a (somewhat) clean build of SMT-RAT. (i.e., you did not screw up the CMake flags, see @ref build_cmake for more information)
19 - You compile either with `CMAKE_BUILD_TYPE=DEBUG` or `DEVELOPER=ON`. This will give additional warnings during compilation and enable assertions during runtime. This will slow down SMT-RAT significantly, but detect errors before an actual crash happens and give a meaningful error message in many cases.
20 
21 If you are unable to solve issue yourself or you find the issue to be an actual bug in SMT-RAT, please do not hesitate to contact us.
22 You can either contact us via email (if you suspect a configuration or usage issue on your side) or create a ticket in our bug tracker (if you suspect an error that is to be fixed by us).
23 The bug tracker is available at http://ths.informatik.rwth-aachen.de/trac/smtrat and is shared with our CArL project.
24 
25 When sending us a mail or creating a ticket, please provide us with:
26 - Your system specifications, including versions of compilers and libraries listed in the dependencies.
27 - The SMT-RAT version (release version of git commit id).
28 - A minimal working example.
29 - A description of what you would expect to happen.
30 - A description of what actually happens.

bool  includeConstants = true

Break down a polynomial into its irreducible factors together with their exponents/multiplicities.

E.g. "x^3 + 4 x^2 + 5 x + 2" factorizes into "(x+1)^2 * (x+2)^1" where "(x+1)", "(x+2)" are the irreducible factors and "2" and "1" are their exponents.

Parameters
includeConstantsOne of those factors is a constant-polynomial (degree 0), which is included by default but can be left out by setting the flag 'includeConstantFlag' to false, e.g., for root computations.
Returns
A map whose keys are the irreducible factors and whose values are the exponents.

Break down a polynomial into its unique, non-constant,irreducible factors without their exponents/multiplicities.

E.g. "3*x^3 + 12*x^2 + 15*x + 6" has the unique, non-constant, irreducible factors "(x+1)", "(x+2)", and a constant factor "3" that is omited.
